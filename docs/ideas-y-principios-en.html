Our Vision

Ideas and Principles

BACK FAQ ELECTION REGULATIONS
"We have the right to share our ideas, to say what we think, and to and to defend the future we want with determination."

Politics

We believe all citizens should choose those who govern us through direct and secret voting, supported by a new constitution that restores authority to the executive, legislative, and judicial powers.

Political Forces

We aspire to a society where political forces are balanced horizontally. We believe in a Law of Political Parties that guarantees transparency in campaigns and finances, where only the people can grant power through voting.

Freedom of Expression

No true democracy can exist without the widest freedom of expression. This freedom cannot depend on political ideologies, religious beliefs, sexual preferences, or economic status.

Economy

We support an economy managed by the people, governed by just laws that incentivize investment, generate jobs, and stop mass migration through real and dignified wages.

Education

Education must be a priority, universal right, and without indoctrination. We seek a secular system that allows each individual to grow materially and spiritually with fairly compensated teachers.

Health

Medical access is a fundamental pillar. We believe in the coexistence of public and private services, dignifying doctors' work with decent pay and eliminating exploitative mechanisms.

Agriculture

We propose a model that gives the farmers back the right to decide about their production and real ownership of their land, eliminating bureaucratic control over what is planted and sold.

Local Security

The military should be minimal, professional, and loyal to the constitution, not to families. Our Ministry of the Interior should protect all people in their democratic and plural daily lives.

Internet

Massive high-speed connection is vital. We reject any control policy over the web and defend the legitimate right of every Cuban to be connected with the rest of the world.

Duties

  • Contribute by example and actions to the health of the organization.
  • Make the best effort to be properly trained.
  • Practice respect and tolerance towards differing political ideas.
  • Pay the voluntary monthly fee.

Rights

  • Receive regular information about activities and finances.
  • Voice and vote to elect the presidency every four years.
  • Voice and vote to approve or disapprove changes in general policies.
  • Participate voluntarily in any action of the movement.

Ing. Eliecer L. Ávila Cicilia
