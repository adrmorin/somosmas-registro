Letter to the Cuban Youth

"I affirm that Cubans can build one of the most modern, participatory, and developed societies of our time."

Our country has everything it needs to embark on the path to well-being. But as a young person, I have often asked myself: What would be better?

Waiting for the Historical Leadership to someday decide to take conscience? Leaving the country like thousands? Becoming an accomplice of the dominant class? Playing the fool and inventing an ephemeral happiness?

I don't feel comfortable with any of these variants. My conscience doesn't stop screaming at me. I have a duty to try to wake up my country before it becomes impossible to catch up with the rest of the world.

Surely I'm not the only one. Thousands of young people struggle with the same doubts. But they have told us we can't... all that is false; the real barriers are in our minds.

We take on the challenge and responsibility: to demonstrate that there are still people who can raise the flag of hope and undertake thePatria's reconstruction.

Peace, development, and life for our people!

Eng. Eliecer L. Avila Cicilia
